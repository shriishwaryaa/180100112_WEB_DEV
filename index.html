INDUS lab

I work under Prof. Vivek Sangwan in the Intelligent Dynamical Ubiquitous Systems Lab which is part of the Mechanical Engineering department in IITB. I joined the lab after the lockdown was imposed and I have learnt a lot in such a short period of time!

I started out with manipulator dynamics and the Euler-Lagrange theorem. Then I simulated motions of links of a manipulator when given the torques exerted on them. Now, I have completed studying about manipulator kinematics, both forward and inverse, dynamics and PD control.

I also learnt about quadcopters through the Aerial Robotics Course by University of Pennsylvania on Coursera. It covered basics about rotation matrices, dynamics, trajectory generation and controls. The course had very interesting videos on swarms.

i-Farmo

This is an Android app development project that I did as an internship during April and May. It is an app to track and monitor pest traps on farms. Can't disclose anything else as of now.

The app uses Firebase Authentication, Realtime-database and RoomDatabase! I built the app using Android Studio. I completed an Android course through the Edufect platform in Summer 2019.

If you wanna get started Android App Development, I would suggest that you should start by reading the book Head First Android Development. It is great for beginners as it explains everything through ample amount of pictures.

AI with python

This is the well-known online HarvardX course "Introduction to Artificial Intelligence with Python". The course was very good and anyone with basic python knowledge is ready to take it.

This course had 9 quizzes with 4 questions each and 12 projects. You need to get a score of 70% or above to pass. It covered topics such as propositional search, knowledge representation, bayesian networks, constraint satisfaction problems, backtracking search, convolutional and recurrent neural networks, naive bayes classification, bag of words model etc.

It also taught me various algorithms such as breadth-first search, depth-first search, A* search, minimax, tf-idf, Q-learning, and KNN classifier etc. As part of the course, I learnt to develop games such as tic tac toe, minesweeper, nim etc.

Conversational chatbot

Conversational chatbot was a project under the Summer of Code conducted by the Web and Coding Club, IITB. I mentored 8 students as part of this project. All my mentees were taught the basics of machine learning, neural netowrks, Firebase, TensorFlow and Android app development.

We built a chatbot that trained on the Cornell movie dialog corpus. The bot was based on a Seq2Seq model. Some of my mentees added an attention mechanism to improve the accuracy / performance of the chatbot. We used tfLite to add our chatbot to the app.
